Writing


Flares

By Danica Kane

When tragedy strikes Joseph's family he soon finds himself on the receiving end of his grief stricken fathers feverish attempts to rekindle his sons fire. But what happens when his father's actions, though pure in intent, land Joseph in terrible danger. It begins with a whisper, a rumour of an elusive summer camp conveniently presenting itself to the blinded man in the form of a rumpled brochure. But before long, friends turn on friends. Children grown old and weary with violence. And all are destined to fall to the flames engulfing them.

Reflection

By Danica Kane

Andy Croft always thought himself a placid boy. At least that's what everybody who knew him said.

“There goes poor Andy, a shy boy, hardley leaves the house,”

“Oh look at him, its no wonder hes all alone, so quiet and timid as he is,”

But when Andy awakes in the woods behind his house his hands caked in blood and no memory of how he got there, he's not so sure anymore. People start disappearing, his friends and enemies like; and each time Andy feels a little more of himself slipping away. Lost among the contents of his closet a dusty ornate mirror starts to weigh on his mind. Cracks are forming, and Andy is terrified to think what might escape when the glass shatters.
